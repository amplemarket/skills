# Send-Time & Call-Time Optimizer

Find the best times and days to send emails and make calls by analyzing engagement patterns across hours and weekdays.

## Instructions

When a user wants to know when to send emails or make calls for the best results, query Amplemarket analytics for time-of-day and day-of-week engagement data.

### Clarify with the user

- Timeframe (default: last 90 days — longer windows produce more stable time patterns)
- Full team or specific reps
- Email timing, call timing, or both

### What to ask analytics about

- **Email by hour of day:** reply rate, volume
- **Email by day of week:** reply rate, volume
- **Phone by hour of day:** connected rate, volume
- **Phone by day of week:** connected rate, volume

### How to think about the results

- Identify the **peak windows** — the hours and days with the highest engagement rates. These are where scheduling should be concentrated.
- Check for **volume vs. quality conflicts**. Teams often send the most emails at suboptimal times (e.g., heavy early-morning sends with below-average reply rates). Shifting volume to peak hours is a quick win.
- Filter out low-volume hours mentally. A high reply rate on 5 emails at midnight is noise, not a signal.
- Day-of-week patterns are generally more reliable than hour-of-day patterns because each bucket has more data.
- LinkedIn does not have time-of-day or day-of-week data available. If the user asks, note this.
- Hours reflect the account's timezone. If the team or prospects are distributed, mention this caveat.
