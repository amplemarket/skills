# Channel Performance Showdown

Compare email, LinkedIn, and phone performance side by side to identify which outreach channels drive the best engagement and meetings.

## Instructions

When a user wants to know which channels are working best, query Amplemarket analytics across all three outreach channels and present a unified comparison.

### Clarify with the user

- Timeframe (default: last 30 days)
- Full team or specific reps/teams

### What to ask analytics about

- **Email:** volume, reply rate, interested rate
- **LinkedIn:** volume by message type (text, voice, video, AI voice), reply rate, interested rate, connection acceptance rate
- **Phone:** calls dialed, connected rate, interested rate
- **Meetings:** booked count (as the shared outcome metric)

Query current period and prior period for week-over-week or month-over-month comparison if the user wants trends.

### How to think about the results

- Normalize by volume. A channel with high rates but tiny volume may not be scalable. Always present both absolute numbers and rates.
- Focus on **interested rate** as the quality metric — it's the most honest signal across channels. Reply rate and connected rate are useful but mean different things per channel.
- Identify the **channel mix** — what percentage of total outreach comes from each channel? Most teams over-index on email. If phone has the highest interested rate but represents 5% of touches, that's an opportunity.
- Compare **LinkedIn message types**. AI voice, voice, video, and text messages can have very different engagement rates. This is a unique insight most teams don't track.
- If a channel has zero activity, the team may not use it. Note this rather than reporting zero performance.
- Meetings are tracked at the sequence level, not per channel. If the user asks which channel drives the most meetings, explain this.
